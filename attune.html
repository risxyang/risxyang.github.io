Attune


Attune is an audio narrative anthology, published by the Yale Daily News. Our first season showcases the work of more than 30 writers, voice actors, producers, and composers. It was produced remotely due to covid-19.

I came up with the concept for this kind of collaborative project at the YDN, led production of our first season, and trained 6 co-producers in sound engineering with Audacity. Attune was described by our editor as "the most ambitious project ever done at the YDN" due to the sheer scope of talent involved.

We published our first 100+ minute season on Spotify, Soundcloud, Anchor, Breaker, Google Podcasts, and a few additional platforms.

Episode one, “Subtle Shifts,” centers on themes of change. It features three short stories.

Episode two, “Memento Vivere,” brings you two audio dramas about the desire to go on living.

Episode three, "Wistful Thinking" (Punning on the phrase Wishful Thinking), brings you three stories about memory. It features two poems and a short story.

Our team also invited collaborations from Yale alumni and connected them with our student writers. Alumni who contributed to this season include Michaela Jill Murphy, known for voicing Toph in Avatar: The Last Airbender, and Bobby Cronin, award winning composer. Both were featured in our second episode. In future seasons, we intend to invite alumni to give panels and masterclasses to our production team as well as the rest of the student body.

We received overwhelming positive feedback from writers, composers, and voice actors about our remote production process, and multiple people have told us our project has brought great meaning to an otherwise very difficult semester.
